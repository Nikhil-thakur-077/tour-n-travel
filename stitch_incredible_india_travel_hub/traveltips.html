Cultural Etiquette

Dress Modestly

When visiting temples or rural areas, cover your shoulders and knees. Carrying a light scarf or shawl is always handy.

Greetings

The traditional 'Namaste' with folded hands is universally appreciated. It’s a sign of deep respect for the divine in everyone.

Shoes Matter

Always remove shoes before entering a home or a place of worship. Look for the designated shoe stand or rows of footwear.

Eating Habits

Eating with your right hand is traditional. If you're unsure, observing locals is the best way to learn the rhythm.
